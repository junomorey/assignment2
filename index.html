    An introduction

    Description of the piece

    As the title might imply, this piece has a dark and mysterious tone to it. The hollow, metallic, distant sounds throughout the song seem to fade in and out, almost like a being breathing in and out. There is not really any melody or concrete rhythm - it is more of a continuous blend of sounds. Especially prominent in the beginning of the song, there is a constant somewhat distorted sound resembling trickling water - like a stream or maybe even underwater. Sublte chimes and rising cymbols varying in intensity  are mixed in to the other sounds, creating a sort of 'shine' in the music.  After about 3 and a half minutes, we begin to hear sounds that might resemble creatures or animals. These consist of high pitched sweeps (which, to me sound similar to calls of a dolphin or whale) and shorther 'chirps', sometimes seeming to complement eachother - like an interaction between multiple creatures. At around the halfway point (5 and a half minutes) There is a buildup in intensity that mysteriously resolves back to the normal state of the song. The frequency of the sweeps and 'animal sounds' increases in the second half of the song. At about 9 and a half minutes, there seems to be some sort of interaction depicted, possibly a fight between some of these 'creatures'  represented with sweeps and croaking sounds. 

    Connections to Past Styles

    Romanticism

    This piece relates to the era of romanticism in the sense that it's a very expressive piece  whose sounds seem to represent a theme in nature, and its dark and mysterious tone could potentially be depicting something supernatural.  One romantic pieces that had strong ties to nature is Schubert's "Der Jungling an der quelle". The piano accompaniment in the first of these two songs, in my view, seems to resemble the trickling of a stream - similar to the water sound effects in "Under the Ice", however in a less direct way.  "Under the Ice" is very different from traditional romantic pieces in the sense that there is no concrete melody or rhythm, only pure sound. 

    My Thoughts

    The story that I hear

    If I were to create a story out of this song, it would be of a person out on a frozen lake  having an out of body experience.  They might be looking up at their body from underneath the ice, in the water. Things are moving in slow motion and the 'breathing' of the sounds represents their own breath. The sweeping 'animal sounds' represent figures in the distant dark water that are trying to communicate with the person, more of these figures appearring throughout the song.
